May.2, 2024

This macro photography assignment was a really fun assignment. I enjoyed using my creativity to create a variety of macro shots, and using different lenses for different shots was an interesting experience. I'll start by talking about my experience with the kit lens, this lens is byfar one of most versitile lens for taking photos; however, this lens is not specifically created for macro photography. Therefore it is a little bit of a struggle to create macro shots with intense depth of field as the aperature doesn't go as low as the others. Next I will talk about the prime lenses, 50mm and 90mm. The 90mm was probably the best macro photography lens I've used to date, the lens is phenominal; it's aperature is perfect, and it zooms in a solid amount to create a good macro effect. On the other hand the 50mm prime lens while being designed for some macro photography and featuring a wide aperature, it doesn't zoom in as much as other lenses like the 90mm prime lens; for this reason, using this lens is a little bit of a pain, it's a constant struggle to find a google angle and get close enough but not too close to maintain focus. This lens overall is great for other types of photography, but it isn't the best for macro in my honest opinion.

June.6, 2024

For this assignment we were asked to create a product photography shot of any product we wanted. I chose to take a photograph of a Dell mouse, as it provided ample opportunity to experiment with a variety of backgrounds and setups. I also thought that it was generally just a cool product to photograph. I went with a black background to enphasize a colorscheme and possibly include a bit of moderness or luxury into my shot. The texture of the background also complimented the smoothness of the mouse. For my lens I went with a 90mm prime, as it provided a good amount of depth of field as well as a clear close up shot of my product. In terms of lighting, I chose to experiment with a variety of lights; in the end I went with a singular light which was aimed a little away from my product. Although, I did experiment with different colored lights, which also gave a good result.
